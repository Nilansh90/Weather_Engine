About Weather Engine

A production-oriented machine learning platform designed to forecast tomorrow's atmospheric conditions through hierarchical inference, feature engineering and automation.

Why Weather Engine Exists

Project vision and engineering motivation.

Traditional weather applications present forecasts without explaining how they are produced.

Weather Engine was developed to explore whether structured historical observations, engineered features and hierarchical machine learning could be combined into a production-style forecasting platform capable of generating explainable, automated and continuously evaluated weather predictions.

This project focuses equally on machine learning, software engineering, automation and data engineering rather than treating forecasting as a single regression problem.

Current Scope

Operational boundary of the current implementation.

Current implementation focuses on five representative Indian cities selected to capture diverse climatic conditions including arid, coastal, metropolitan and heat-belt environments.

The forecasting pipeline operates at daily resolution using approximately fifteen years of historical observations and integrates Numerical Weather Prediction where atmospheric physics exceeds the practical limits of tabular machine learning.

Beyond Weather Prediction

Weather Engine demonstrates how machine learning, feature engineering, relational databases, automation, hierarchical inference and modern web technologies can be integrated into a single production-style forecasting platform.

Rather than being designed as a standalone predictive model, the platform was engineered as an end-to-end machine learning system where every component contributes to reliable, explainable and continuously evaluated forecasts.
